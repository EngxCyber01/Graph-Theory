Essential Concepts at a Glance

Core Definitions

  • Binomial Coefficient: C(n, k) = n!/(k!(n-k)!) - ways to choose k from n
  • Notation: (n k) or C(n, k) or nCk
  • Symmetry: C(n, k) = C(n, n-k)
  • Boundaries: C(n, 0) = C(n, n) = 1

Key Formulas

Formula Expression Use
Pascal's Identity C(n,k) = C(n-1,k-1) + C(n-1,k) Recursive computation
Binomial Theorem (a+b)ⁿ = Σ C(n,k)aⁿ⁻ᵏbᵏ Expansion of powers
Sum Property Σ C(n,k) = 2ⁿ Total subsets count
Alternating Sum Σ (-1)ᵏC(n,k) = 0 For n > 0

Pascal's Triangle (Quick Reference)

Row 0:                    1
Row 1:                  1   1
Row 2:                1   2   1
Row 3:              1   3   3   1
Row 4:            1   4   6   4   1
Row 5:          1   5  10  10   5   1
Row 6:        1   6  15  20  15   6   1

Pattern: Each entry = sum of two entries above it

Important Identities

  • Vandermonde: C(m+n, r) = Σ C(m,k)×C(n,r-k)
  • Hockey Stick: Σ C(k,r) = C(n+1,r+1) for k=r to n
  • Sum of Squares: Σ C(n,k)² = C(2n,n)
  • Symmetry: C(n,k) = C(n,n-k)

Quick Examples

Example 1: Basic Calculation

C(6, 2) = 6!/(2!×4!) = (6×5)/2 = 15

Example 2: Binomial Expansion

(x + 2)⁴ = x⁴ + 8x³ + 24x² + 32x + 16

Coefficients: 1, 4, 6, 4, 1 (from Row 4 of Pascal's Triangle)

Adjusted by powers of 2: 1, 4×2=8, 6×4=24, 4×8=32, 1×16=16

Example 3: Subset Count

Set with 7 elements has 2⁷ = 128 total subsets

Subsets of size 3: C(7,3) = 35

Common Applications

Application Formula Example
Choosing a committee C(n, k) 5 from 10: C(10,5) = 252

Polynomial expansion Binomial Theorem Expand (x+y)ⁿ
Counting paths C(n+k, k) Grid paths

Computation Tips

  1. Use symmetry: C(100,98) = C(100,2) is easier to compute
  2. Cancel before multiplying: Simplify fractions first
  3. Use Pascal's Triangle: For small values, read directly
  4. Multiplicative form: C(n,k) = C(n,k-1) × (n-k+1)/k

Quick Reference

  • C(n, 0) = C(n, n) = 1 always
  • C(n, 1) = C(n, n-1) = n
  • C(n, 2) = n(n-1)/2
  • Sum of row n in Pascal's Triangle = 2ⁿ
  • Middle term is largest: C(n, n/2) is maximum
